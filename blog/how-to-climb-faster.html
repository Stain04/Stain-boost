Being stuck in Iron, Bronze, or Silver is one of the most frustrating experiences in League of Legends. You feel like you're playing well, your team keeps losing, and the LP just won't come. Sound familiar? As the #1 Master Yi on the Middle East server with hundreds of boosting orders completed across all ranks, I can tell you that low elo is almost always a skill and mindset problem — not a team problem.

These 10 tips are the exact framework I give to players who want to know how to climb elo on their own. Apply them consistently and you will see results.

Tip 1: Lock In a 2–3 Champion Pool

Tip 01

Stop Playing 20 Champions

The single biggest mistake low elo players make is playing too many champions. Every time you load into a game on a champion you've played 10 times, you're starting at a mechanical and knowledge deficit compared to someone who has played that champion 200 times. Pick 2–3 champions maximum, play them until you understand every interaction, every matchup, every power spike, and every win condition. The champion pool should cover 2 roles so you're not auto-filled into an unfamiliar position with an unfamiliar champ.

For climbing in low elo specifically, prioritize champions that don't rely on your team. Snowball carries, strong duelers, and champions with reliable engage or self-peel are ideal. We go deeper on champion picks in our best champions for solo queue article.

Tip 2: Focus on Objectives, Not Kills

Tip 02

Kills Don't Win Games — Towers and Dragons Do

Low elo players chase kills obsessively. High elo players chase objectives. Every kill is an opportunity cost — you could be taking a tower, securing Dragon, or setting up for Baron. Get into the habit of asking yourself after every kill: "What objective can I convert right now?" If the answer is nothing, consider whether the fight was even worth taking. Games are won by accumulating structural advantages, not by padding your KDA.

Tip 3: Master Wave Management

Tip 03

CS Is Free Gold That Most Low Elo Players Leave on the Table

A full wave of minions at 20 minutes is worth roughly 300–400 gold — equivalent to a kill. Low elo players routinely miss 30–50% of available CS throughout a game, bleeding thousands of gold. Learn the basics: last-hitting under tower, slow pushing before roaming, freezing a wave near your turret when you're behind, and crashing waves before objectives spawn. Wave management is one of the highest-leverage skills you can develop because it generates gold regardless of what your team is doing.

Tip 4: Know Your Win Condition Every Game

Tip 04

Play With a Clear Game Plan

Before each game, identify your champion's win condition. Are you a hard carry who needs to scale? Push for a fast game and deny the enemy scaling. Are you a team-fight powerhouse? Group early, force fights, and avoid split-push scenarios. Are you a split pusher? Apply side-lane pressure and only group when your team needs you. Playing without a clear win condition leads to reactive, reactive play — doing whatever seems most urgent rather than executing a deliberate strategy.

Tip 5: Protect Your Mental — Avoid Tilt

Tip 05

Tilt Is the Number One LP Destroyer in Low Elo

Every serious player has had the experience: you lose one game, get frustrated, queue again, play worse, lose another, spiral. Tilt is responsible for more LP loss than any single mechanical weakness. Implement a hard rule: if you lose two games in a row, stop playing ranked for at least an hour. Go for a walk, play ARAM, watch a replay — anything other than queueing again. Returning to ranked after a break resets your mental state and dramatically improves your decision-making quality.

Mental tip: Mute all chat at the start of every game. You cannot control what your teammates type, but you can control whether it affects your focus. Use pings to communicate — they're faster and less emotionally charged than text anyway.

Tip 6: Learn from Your Replays

Tip 06

Watch Your Losses, Not Your Wins

Most players remember wins fondly and attribute losses to teammates. Replay review forces you to confront the truth. After a loss, pull up the replay and watch it with one specific question in mind: "What could I have done differently at each major decision point?" Look for: missed CS windows, poor positioning before teamfights, incorrect recalls, overextending, or ignoring available objectives. You only need to find one or two things per replay — make a note and focus on not repeating that mistake in the next session.

Tip 7: Limit Your Daily Sessions

Tip 07

Quality Over Quantity — Stop Marathon Grinding

Playing 10 games in a row is almost never productive. Fatigue degrades decision-making, reaction time, and emotional regulation. High-quality sessions of 3–5 focused games will generate more LP than 10 exhausted games. If you're aiming to climb faster in League of Legends, structure your ranked time intentionally — play when you're fresh, stop when you're tired, and always stop after two consecutive losses regardless of where you are in the session.

Tip 8: Communicate Efficiently

Tip 08

Use Pings Effectively — Not to Flame

Communication in low elo is mostly negative — blame pings, spam pings, and flame in chat. Be the player who uses the ping system constructively. Ping when an enemy goes missing. Ping when Dragon or Baron is spawning. Ping "on my way" when you're rotating to assist a teammate. Ping "retreat" when you see a collapsing rotation on the minimap. Good, timely pings win more games than you'd expect because they provide information and coordination that low elo teams otherwise lack completely.

Tip 9: Track Your Mistakes, Not Your Teammates'

Tip 09

You Can Only Control Your Own Play

This is the hardest mindset shift for most players to make, but it's the most important one for climbing out of Silver or any low elo bracket. Your teammates' mistakes are outside your control. Your own mistakes are not. Every game, regardless of outcome, ask yourself: "Did I CS efficiently? Did I die to easily-avoided damage? Did I make the right macro call?" Build the habit of self-audit and your gameplay will improve game over game, while players who blame teammates will remain stuck indefinitely.

Tip 10

Putting It All Together

These 10 tips aren't secrets — they're fundamentals that high elo players apply consistently while low elo players ignore them in favor of chasing kills and blaming teammates. If you implement even half of these changes starting today, you will notice your win rate trending upward within a week.
