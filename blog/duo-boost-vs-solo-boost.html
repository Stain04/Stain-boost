When you go to order a boost, one of the first choices you'll face is Solo vs Duo. The price is different, the experience is different, and the right answer depends entirely on what you care about most. This guide breaks it down clearly so you can decide in under two minutes.

⚔️ Solo Boost
  • Booster logs into your account
  • You don't need to be online
  • Credentials are shared temporarily
  • Lower price per division
  • Faster completion
  • You're offline the whole time
🤝 Duo Boost
  • You play on your own account
  • Booster plays on their account
  • You queue together as a team
  • Higher price per division
  • Slightly slower (requires coordination)
  • You can watch and learn in real time

How Each Type Works

Solo Boost

With a solo boost, you share your account credentials with the booster. They log in — using a VPN matched to your region and offline mode enabled — and play ranked games on your account until the order is completed. You don't need to be online or involved at all. You can check back when it's done and your rank has moved.

This is the most common type of boost. It's cheaper, faster, and requires zero involvement from you during the process. The only thing you're exchanging is temporary access to your account.

Duo Boost

With a duo boost, your account never leaves your hands. The booster adds you on their high-elo account, and you queue together as a duo — you play on your main, they play on theirs. Because you're in the same lobby, the booster can carry games from the same team rather than playing on your account. You experience the games directly.

This type costs more because it requires coordination and the booster can't play multiple sessions independently — they're waiting on your availability to queue. But your credentials stay private and you get to play alongside a genuinely elite player.

Price Difference

Duo boosting costs roughly 30–40% more per division than solo boosting. This reflects the additional coordination required — the booster is available only when you're available to queue, which limits how many hours per day they can work on your order. Solo boosting allows the booster to work at any time, which speeds up completion and reduces the per-division cost.

Which Is Safer?

Both types are safe when done correctly — but they carry different types of risk.

Solo boosting involves account sharing, which is against Riot's Terms of Service. The risk is managed through VPN (matching your region), offline mode, human-only play, and controlled session lengths. With these precautions in place, the practical ban risk is very low — StainBoost has completed 500+ solo orders with zero bans.

Duo boosting technically involves no account sharing. You're playing on your own account alongside a high-elo friend, which Riot has no mechanism to prohibit. The ToS risk is essentially zero. The only theoretical risk is if Riot were to flag unusual win rates when queued with the same duo partner repeatedly — but this is an extreme edge case not seen in practice.

Bottom line on safety: If account security is your top priority and you don't want to share credentials under any circumstances, Duo is the clear choice. If you're comfortable with the standard solo boost safety protocols and want the lower price, Solo is fine.

Which Is Faster?

Solo boosting is faster in almost every case. The booster can play whenever they're available — morning, afternoon, overnight — without waiting for you. A 10-win solo boost can often be completed in 2–3 days of active play.

Duo boosting is limited to hours when both you and the booster are online at the same time. If you have 2–3 hours available per day, a 10-win duo boost takes 4–6 days on average. For players with busy schedules, this can stretch out significantly.

If you need the boost completed by a specific date (end of season, upcoming event), solo is the more reliable choice for hitting a deadline.

Who Should Pick Which

Choose Solo If...
  • You want the lowest possible price
  • You don't want to be online during the boost
  • You have a deadline to hit
  • You're comfortable sharing credentials temporarily
  • You want it done as fast as possible
Choose Duo If...
  • You never want to share your password
  • You want to play and learn at the same time
  • You enjoy the experience of queuing with a pro
  • Minimising ToS risk is your top priority
  • You have a flexible schedule

The most common choice is solo — it's cheaper, faster, and the account security concerns are well-managed with proper protocols. Duo is the right pick for players who specifically don't want to hand over credentials, or who want to actively participate in the boosted games.
